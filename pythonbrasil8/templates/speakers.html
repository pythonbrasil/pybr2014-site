Fernando Perez

{% blocktrans %} Fernando Perez is a research scientist at the UC Berkeley Brain Imaging Center. After a PhD in Physics from the University of Colorado, Boulder and postdoctoral work in Applied Mathematics on numerical algorithms, his research moved into the development of tools to effectively extract and communicate insight from computation and data. He is the creator and lead of the IPython environment for interactive computing, as well as a contributor to a number of scientific Python projects. He is a member of the Python Software Foundation as well as a founding board member of the Numfocus Foundation. He received the 2012 Award for the Advancement of Free Software from the Free Software Foundation and is a founding investigator of the Berkeley Institute for Data Science, created in 2013. {% endblocktrans %}

Twitter: @fperez_org
